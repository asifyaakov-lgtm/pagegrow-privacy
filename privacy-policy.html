Privacy Policy

PageGrow — Facebook Page Inviter  ·  Last updated: March 2026

PageGrow is a Chrome extension that helps Facebook page owners invite people who reacted to their posts to follow their page. This Privacy Policy explains what data the extension accesses and how it is handled.

1. Data We Do NOT Collect

PageGrow does not collect, transmit, or store any personal data on external servers. Specifically, we do not collect:

— Your name, email address, or any account information
— Your Facebook profile data or friends list
— Browsing history or web activity
— Any information about the people you invite

2. Data Stored Locally

The extension stores the following data locally in your browser using Chrome's built-in storage API. This data never leaves your device:

— Your extension settings (delay duration, max invites per run)
— Run statistics (number of invites sent, posts scanned)
— Your license key (to verify your subscription status)
— A flag indicating whether your free trial has been used

3. License Verification

When you activate a license key, the extension sends that key to Gumroad's API (api.gumroad.com) solely to verify its validity. No other data is sent. Gumroad's own privacy policy applies to this interaction: gumroad.com/privacy.

4. Facebook Interaction

PageGrow operates on facebook.com by reading the page's interface to locate reaction buttons and clicking the "Invite" button on your behalf. It does not read, copy, or transmit any content from Facebook — it only automates actions you would otherwise perform manually.

5. Third-Party Services

PageGrow does not use analytics services, advertising networks, or any other third-party services that collect user data.

6. Children's Privacy

PageGrow is not directed at children under the age of 13 and does not knowingly collect any information from children.

7. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any changes will be reflected on this page with an updated date.
